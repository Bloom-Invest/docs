## Available Collections

### Top Momentum Stocks

**Investment Strategy:** Ride stocks with strong recent price performance and positive technical indicators

**Screening Criteria:**
- High relative strength (outperforming market)
- Strong price momentum (3-month performance > 15%)
- RSI between 50-70 (not overbought)
- Above 50-day and 200-day moving averages
- Higher-than-average volume

**Ideal For:** Active traders and growth investors looking for trending stocks

**Example AI Reasoning:**
> **NVDA - Nvidia Corporation**
>
> **Why Featured:**
> - 180% gain over past 3 months, massively outperforming S&P 500
> - Strong technical setup: RSI at 62, above all moving averages
> - Earnings momentum: Beat estimates by 18% last quarter
> - Catalyst: Data center AI accelerator demand exceeding supply
> - Institutional buying: Volume 2.3x average
>
> **Risks:** High valuation (P/E: 45), potential for pullback after strong run

### Undervalued Gems

**Investment Strategy:** Find quality companies trading below intrinsic value with strong fundamentals

**Screening Criteria:**
- P/E ratio below sector average
- Price-to-book < 3
- Revenue growth > 10%
- Positive earnings growth
- Low debt-to-equity ratio

**Ideal For:** Value investors seeking bargains with catalysts for re-rating

**Example AI Reasoning:**
> **INTC - Intel Corporation**
>
> **Why Featured:**
> - Trading at P/E of 12 vs sector average of 28 (57% discount)
> - Revenue growing 15% YoY despite valuation compression
> - Catalyst: Foundry business winning new customers, IRA funding
> - Margin expansion: Operating margins improving to 20%
> - Dividend yield: 2.8% while reinvesting in growth
>
> **Risks:** Competitive pressure from TSMC, execution risk on roadmap

### Dividend Aristocrats

**Investment Strategy:** Reliable income from companies with long track records of dividend growth

**Screening Criteria:**
- 10+ years of consecutive dividend increases
- Dividend yield > 2.5%
- Payout ratio < 75% (sustainable)
- Positive free cash flow
- Investment-grade credit rating

**Ideal For:** Income investors and retirees seeking stable, growing passive income

**Example AI Reasoning:**
> **JNJ - Johnson & Johnson**
>
> **Why Featured:**
> - 62 consecutive years of dividend increases (true aristocrat)
> - Current yield: 3.1% with 5.8% annual growth rate
> - Payout ratio: 48% (very safe, room to grow)
> - Diversified revenue: Pharma, MedTech, Consumer Health
> - AAA credit rating: Ultimate financial strength
>
> **Risks:** Patent cliffs on key drugs, litigation overhang

### Sector Leaders

**Investment Strategy:** Invest in best-in-class companies dominating their industries

**Screening Criteria:**
- #1 or #2 market share in their sector
- Higher margins than competitors
- Strong brand moat (pricing power)
- Consistent revenue and earnings growth
- Industry tailwinds

**Ideal For:** Long-term investors wanting quality compounders

**Example AI Reasoning:**
> **MSFT - Microsoft Corporation**
>
> **Why Featured:**
> - Dominant cloud provider (#2 globally, growing faster than #1)
> - Operating margins: 42% vs sector average 25%
> - Moat: Enterprise lock-in via Azure, Office 365, Teams
> - AI leadership: Copilot integration across entire product suite
> - Revenue growth: 16% with expanding margins
>
> **Risks:** Regulatory scrutiny, high valuation (P/E: 35)

- Leadership in emerging technologies

**Ideal For:** Growth investors with higher risk tolerance seeking outsized returns

> - Leading EV manufacturer with 20% global market share
> - FSD (Full Self-Driving) approaching L3 autonomy

### Defensive Holdings

**Investment Strategy:** Preserve capital with low-volatility, recession-resistant businesses

**Screening Criteria:**
- Beta < 0.8 (less volatile than market)
- Essential products/services (recession-proof)
- Consistent cash flows (10+ years)
- Strong balance sheet (low debt)
- Stable dividend history

**Ideal For:** Conservative investors and those nearing retirement

**Example AI Reasoning:**
> **PG - Procter & Gamble**
>
> **Why Featured:**
> - Beta: 0.42 (58% less volatile than S&P 500)
> - Essential consumer staples: Tide, Pampers, Gillette
> - 68 years of consecutive dividend payments
> - Revenue stability: Never had negative YoY growth
> - Pricing power: Can pass costs to consumers
>
> **Risks:** Slow growth (3-4% annually), currency headwinds

## How Collections Are Generated

<Steps>
  <Step title="Daily Screening">
    Every market day, Bloom screens thousands of stocks using quantitative criteria for each collection strategy
  </Step>

  <Step title="Data Analysis">
    AI analyzes multiple data sources for qualifying stocks:
    - Fundamentals (P/E, revenue growth, margins, cash flow)
    - Technicals (RSI, moving averages, volume, momentum)
    - Earnings (estimates, surprises, guidance, transcript sentiment)
    - News & sentiment (analyst ratings, insider trading, catalysts)
  </Step>

  <Step title="AI Reasoning Generation">
    GPT-4o generates investment thesis for each stock explaining:
    - Why it qualifies for the collection
    - Key catalysts and growth drivers
    - Relevant risks and concerns
    - Valuation context vs peers
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ranking & Selection">
    Stocks ranked by:
    - Strength of investment thesis
    - Quality of data signals
    - Risk-adjusted returns potential
    - Diversification within collection
  </Step>

  <Step title="Daily Update">
    Collections refreshed every market day with new opportunities
  </Step>
</Steps>

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Are Daily Ideas buy recommendations?" icon="circle-question">
    **No.** Daily Ideas are curated opportunities for further research, not final buy recommendations.

    Always conduct your own due diligence:
    - Review company fundamentals and financials
    - Assess your risk tolerance and investment goals
    - Consider position sizing and portfolio context
    - Consult with a financial advisor if needed
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How often are collections updated?" icon="clock">
    Collections are updated **every market day** (Monday-Friday when US markets are open).

    Stocks may move between collections or drop out as market conditions change.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I see historical collections?" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    Yes, you can browse past collections to track performance and see which strategies work best.

    Navigate to Collections → History to view previous days' picks and track outcomes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why did a stock disappear from a collection?" icon="eye-slash">
    Stocks may drop out if they no longer meet screening criteria:
    - Momentum stocks: Lost relative strength or became overbought
    - Undervalued: Price increased, no longer cheap
    - Dividends: Cut or suspended dividend
    - Defensive: Volatility increased significantly

    This is normal and helps keep collections fresh and relevant.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I request custom collections?" icon="plus">
    Power users can use the AI Assistant to create custom collections:

    **Example prompts:**
    - "Create a collection of biotech stocks with upcoming FDA decisions"
    - "Find tech stocks trading below 20 P/E with 20%+ revenue growth"
    - "Show me REITs with yields above 5% and low payout ratios"

    The AI will screen stocks and provide reasoning for each match.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
